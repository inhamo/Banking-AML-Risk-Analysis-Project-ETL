# Banking Departments: Roles & Information Needs

## 1. Retail Banking
Retail Banking serves individual customers and manages the bank’s public-facing operations.

### What They Do
- Open/manage accounts (savings, cheque, fixed deposits)
- Issue personal/home/vehicle loans
- Provide debit/credit cards
- Operate branches and customer service
- Oversee daily customer interactions

### Why They Exist
To grow customer numbers, increase product usage, and generate revenue from the mass market.

### Information They Need
- New customers (daily, weekly, monthly)
- Customer churn and retention
- Product penetration (cards, loans, savings)
- Average products per customer
- Branch and regional performance
- Customer lifetime value (CLV)


## 2. Credit Risk
Credit Risk ensures the bank doesn’t lose money from customers who cannot repay loans.

### What They Do
- Assess and approve/decline loan applications
- Assign internal risk scores
- Monitor early delinquencies and missed payments
- Manage collections and recoveries
- Maintain credit policies

### Why They Exist
To keep the loan book healthy and prevent losses due to defaults.

### Information They Need
- Loan application counts and approval rates
- Customer credit scores
- Repayment history
- Past-due amounts (1/30/60/90+ days)
- Non-performing loan (NPL) ratios
- Risk grades, PD, LGD
- Portfolio-level loan trends


## 3. Finance / CFO
Finance manages the entire financial health, stability, and compliance of the bank.

### What They Do
- Track revenue, expenses, and profitability
- Produce financial statements (P&L, balance sheet)
- Forecast budgets and performance
- Ensure compliance with IFRS/Basel
- Oversee taxes, capital, and regulatory reporting

### Why They Exist
To keep the bank financially stable, profitable, and compliant.

### Information They Need
- Net interest income
- Fee income
- Cost-to-income ratio
- Product/customer profitability
- Liquidity positions
- Operational costs
- Capital adequacy metrics


## 4. Marketing & Growth
Marketing attracts new customers and increases product usage.

### What They Do
- Run digital and branch campaigns
- Manage customer segmentation
- Track acquisition and retention trends
- Communicate offers and brand messaging
- Analyse customer behaviour

### Why They Exist
To grow the customer base and deepen relationships with existing customers.

### Information They Need
- MoM/YoY new customer growth
- Campaign performance data
- Customer segmentation profiles
- Product adoption rates
- Churn indicators
- Lifetime value and engagement metrics


## 5. Digital Banking & IT
Digital Banking manages online platforms, mobile apps, and core banking systems.

### What They Do
- Maintain mobile/online banking platforms
- Ensure app stability and security
- Oversee transaction systems
- Manage integrations and APIs
- Handle cybersecurity and system monitoring

### Why They Exist
To provide fast, secure, reliable digital experiences for customers.

### Information They Need
- App usage and login frequency
- Transaction success/failure rates
- Downtime and incident logs
- Error codes and failure causes
- Digital adoption by customer segment
- Fraud detection signals


## 6. Compliance & AML
Compliance protects the bank from criminal activity, sanctions, and regulatory penalties.

### What They Do
- Monitor suspicious transactions
- Conduct customer KYC verification
- Handle AML flagging and SAR reporting
- Manage sanctions screening
- Ensure adherence to regulations

### Why They Exist
To prevent fraud, money laundering, fines, and legal breaches.

### Information They Need
- KYC documentation and status
- Real-time transaction monitoring data
- Customer risk scoring
- Watchlist/PEP screening hits
- Suspicious activity patterns
- Source-of-funds information


## 7. Treasury & ALM (Asset-Liability Management)
Treasury manages liquidity, interest rate risks, and overall stability of the bank’s balance sheet.

### What They Do
- Manage cash, deposits, and funding
- Oversee interest rate exposure
- Balance loans vs deposits
- Set internal transfer pricing (FTP)
- Invest excess cash prudently

### Why They Exist
To ensure the bank always has enough liquidity and remains financially resilient.

### Information They Need
- Daily liquidity levels
- Deposit inflows/outflows
- Loan issuance volumes
- Interest rate trends
- Net Interest Margin (NIM)
- Maturity profiles of assets/liabilities
- Funding costs and buffers
